# Flash Answers

Flash Answers is a fast-response mode that delivers answers at up to 10x the usual speed. When you need a quick answer, a first draft, or rapid iteration and don't need step-by-step reasoning, Flash Answers gets you there faster. It generates over 1,000 tokens (roughly 750 words) per second, so responses feel almost instant.

<SectionTab as="h1" sectionId="how-it-works">How it works</SectionTab>

Flash Answers prioritizes speed over reasoning depth. Instead of working through a chain-of-thought process, it gives you a direct answer as quickly as possible.

Le Chat uses Flash Answers automatically in certain features. For example, [Deep Research](/le-chat/research-analysis/deep-research) relies on it to generate full reports at speed once the research phase is complete.

<SectionTab as="h1" sectionId="how-to-activate">How to activate</SectionTab>

1. Click the `Flash` button at the bottom of the chat window.
2. Type your message and send it as usual.

Flash Answers stays active for all subsequent messages in the conversation until you turn it off. Click the `Flash` button again to switch back to the standard model.

<SectionTab as="h1" sectionId="when-to-use">When to use Flash Answers</SectionTab>

Flash Answers works best when speed matters more than depth:

- **Quick factual questions**: look up a definition, check a date, or get a concise explanation during a meeting.
- **Short-form content generation**: draft a quick email, write a social media post, or outline talking points.
- **Iterative brainstorming**: generate and refine ideas rapidly without waiting between responses.
- **First drafts**: get a rough version on screen fast, then switch to the standard model or [Canvas](/le-chat/content-creation/canvas) to polish it.

<SectionTab as="h1" sectionId="limitations">Limitations</SectionTab>

Flash Answers trades reasoning depth for speed. It isn't the best choice for tasks that require multi-step logic, nuanced analysis, or careful evaluation of trade-offs. For those, use [Think mode](/le-chat/conversation/think-mode), which applies chain-of-thought reasoning to work through complex problems step by step.

:::tip
If your question has a straightforward answer, use Flash Answers. If you'd want to see the model's reasoning before trusting the answer, use Think mode instead.
:::

<SectionTab as="h1" sectionId="related-features">Related features</SectionTab>

- **[Deep Research](/le-chat/research-analysis/deep-research)**: automated multi-source web research with structured reports (uses Flash Answers for fast generation).
