:::

<SectionTab as="h1" sectionId="fine-tuning-vs-prompting">Fine-tuning vs. prompting</SectionTab>

Start with prompt engineering. It's faster and requires no training data. Switch to fine-tuning when you need better performance on a specialized task.

<SectionTab as="h2" variant="secondary" sectionId="benefits-of-prompting">Benefits of prompting</SectionTab>

- Works out of the box with generic models (zero-shot capability)
- No training data required
- Easy to update for new workflows and rapid prototyping

Explore prompting techniques in our [prompting documentation](completion/prompting_capabilities).

<SectionTab as="h2" variant="secondary" sectionId="benefits-of-fine-tuning">Benefits of fine-tuning</SectionTab>

- Significantly better performance than prompting alone
- Often outperforms larger models: faster and more cost-effective with shorter prompts
- Better alignment through task-specific training
- Teaches the model new facts, advanced tools, and complex workflows

<SectionTab as="h2" variant="secondary" sectionId="fine-tuning-vs-prompting">Common use cases</SectionTab>

- Customize response format and tone
- Specialize models for specific domains or topics
- Distill knowledge from stronger models
- Reproduce complex prompt behavior without the prompt overhead
- Reduce costs and latency with efficient fine-tuned models
